Company history

So, the reason I started my company making ergonomic keyboards is because I used to experience a lot of pain and discomfort in my hands and wrists after typing for long periods of time on a regular keyboard. I did some research and discovered ergonomic keyboards, which are designed to reduce strain and improve comfort during extended typing sessions. The problem was, I found that the options on the market were pretty limited and often too expensive. So, with my background in engineering and product design, I decided to create my own company focused on designing and manufacturing high-quality, ergonomic keyboards that were more affordable and accessible to everyone. I spent a lot of time testing different materials and designs, and working with ergonomics experts to make sure my keyboards were the best they could be. And finally, after months of hard work, I launched my company and released my first line of ergonomic keyboards, which quickly gained a following among professionals who valued their health and comfort. Since then, my company has grown and expanded to include a range of ergonomic accessories, like wrist rests and ergonomic mice. And today, we're recognized as a leader in the ergonomic keyboard market, with a reputation for quality, innovation, and affordability.

Why? Ortholinear Keyboards.

Ortholinear keyboards are designed with the keys arranged in a grid, rather than the staggered layout found on traditional keyboards. This layout can be beneficial for a few reasons, even though ortholinear keyboards typically have fewer keys.

Firstly, the ortholinear layout makes it easier to reach keys accurately and consistently, since the keys are arranged in straight rows and columns. This can result in a more efficient typing experience, with fewer errors and less strain on the hands and fingers.

Secondly, the ortholinear layout can make it easier to use multiple layers of key functions, allowing you to access more commands and shortcuts without cluttering up the keyboard with extra keys. This can be especially useful for programmers, gamers, or anyone who needs to use a lot of different keyboard commands in their work.

Finally, some people simply find the ortholinear layout more comfortable to type on, since it can reduce the amount of hand and finger movement required to reach different keys. This can be especially beneficial for those with repetitive strain injuries or other hand and wrist issues.

So, while ortholinear keyboards may have fewer keys than traditional keyboards, their unique layout and potential benefits make them a popular choice for many users.

Split or Not to split?

According to research, split keyboards can be beneficial for those who type frequently or for long periods of time. One of the main advantages of split keyboards is that they allow each hand to be positioned independently, reducing strain and tension on the hands, wrists, and forearms.

This ergonomic benefit can be especially useful for people with repetitive strain injuries or other hand and wrist issues. Split keyboards can also encourage a more natural and comfortable typing posture, with the shoulders relaxed and the arms positioned at a more natural angle, which can help reduce strain on the neck, shoulders, and back.

In addition to these ergonomic benefits, split keyboards can be more customizable and versatile than traditional keyboards. Many split keyboards come with programmable keys or multiple layers of key functions, allowing users to create shortcuts and macros that can save time and improve productivity.

Overall, according to the research, split keyboards can offer a more comfortable and ergonomic typing experience for those who spend a lot of time typing.
